Module 3

Language Ideology and Power

This module explores how language judgments are connected to social hierarchy and power.

Key Ideas

  • Standard language ideology can make one variety appear naturally superior.
  • Accent discrimination is often linked to class, ethnicity, and education.
  • Language judgments are rarely neutral.

Mini Reading

When people describe a variety as 'proper', 'broken', 'educated', or 'unprofessional', they are not only describing grammar. They are often reproducing social beliefs about whose language has authority.

Reflection Task

Discuss whether the idea of 'correct English' is neutral or socially constructed.
