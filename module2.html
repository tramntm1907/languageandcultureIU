Module 2

World Englishes and Global Englishes

This module examines English as a language used, adapted, and owned by diverse communities.

Key Ideas

  • English is pluricentric.
  • Intelligibility is more important than imitation.
  • Local Englishes reflect local histories and identities.

Mini Reading

Global Englishes challenges the assumption that native-speaker English should be the only model. In many international contexts, effective communication depends on accommodation, clarity, and respect for diverse accents and varieties.

Reflection Task

Find one example of localized English from Vietnamese youth culture, K-drama, gaming, TikTok, or music.
