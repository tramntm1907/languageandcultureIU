Module 4

Minority Languages and Revitalization

This module introduces language endangerment, preservation, and community-based revitalization.

Key Ideas

  • Minority language preservation involves both policy and community agency.
  • Language vitality depends on intergenerational transmission and domains of use.
  • Digital storytelling can make marginalized voices more visible.

Mini Reading

A language survives not only through documentation but also through meaningful use in family, education, media, ceremonies, and public life. Revitalization is strongest when communities are represented as active knowledge holders rather than passive recipients of support.

Reflection Task

Choose one minority language in Vietnam and identify its speakers, location, current challenges, and preservation efforts.
